My Story

Hello! My name is Baptiste. I am a 31 year old French native now living near Portland, Oregon.
My first work experience was in auto body repair and painting.
I love to travel as well as finding different jobs that utilize my talents. So, I left my job and started to travel throughout France, providing new and different work experiences.

In the South of France, I worked at a ski resort, assisting in equipment rentals.
And in the East, I was a wood painter for a company that produced custom display pieces used for animation by prestigious clients like Chanel, Guerlain, Longchamp, Focal, DS, etc.
And later, a handyman- doing whatever that was necessary- at a hotel in the French Alpes.

In late 2018 I traveled within Australia before embarking on a 10 day cruise around New Zealand. This is where I met my future husband, Kirk.
In early 2019 I traveled to Oregon for the first time to spend more time with Kirk. While he was at work, I attended English classes at Concordia University to better my language skills. After 3 months I had to return to France, where I found a job as a powder painter at a door factory.

I was supposed to come back in 2020 but COVID happened and all the borders closed.
So I remained in France and worked at the door factory. After nearly two years, I needed a change in my life.

In 2021 I made the big decision to start professional training within a 5 month bootcamp to become a web developer.
I wouldn't say that bootcamp was easy for me, because it wasn't. In fact I left that bootcamp doubting in my ability to code at all.
Without grasping the basics, it didnt seem like a good idea to continue on this path.
For 6 months I didnt write a line of code. I was really frustrated, but I didn't want to just give up.

Once the borders reopened I returned to the US in late 2021. Ready to restart, I purchased online training from a French youtuber in February and started from zero. This time, focusing on and learning the basics: HTML, CSS and JS.
I am not a fullstack developer yet, but I am proud of what I can do so far, and not ashamed to say I am a junior web developer.
Today I'm happily motivated to continue this journey.
